## How equipment categories work

Every piece of equipment in ChairPulse is assigned to one of 30 categories. Categories determine:

- Which maintenance task recommendations ChairPulse suggests from the master catalog
- Which compliance requirements are automatically applied based on your jurisdiction
- How equipment is grouped in filter dropdowns on the [Equipment list](/equipment/overview)
- Which SOP suggestions are available for the equipment model

When you [add equipment](/equipment/adding-equipment), the category is the first selection you make in the wizard. ChairPulse then filters manufacturers and models based on your chosen category.

## Sub-categories: Dental Chairs & Delivery Units

The **Dental Chairs & Delivery Units** category is unique because it supports sub-categories. A dental operatory often consists of a system with multiple components. ChairPulse lets you register these as a combined system or as individual components.

| Sub-category | Value | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Dental Chair** | `dental_chair` | The patient seating unit, including hydraulic or electric positioning mechanisms. |
| **Delivery Unit** | `delivery_unit` | The instrument delivery system, including handpiece connections, syringes, and controls. |
| **Chair & Delivery Unit (Combined)** | `combo_chair_delivery` | A combined unit where the chair and delivery system are integrated as one piece of equipment. |
| **Operatory Light** | `operatory_light` | The overhead examination light mounted on the chair or ceiling. |
| **Assistant's Instrumentation** | `assistant_instrumentation` | The assistant-side instrument panel, including suction, air/water syringe, and curing light holder. |

### System equipment

When you select a model in the **Dental Chairs & Delivery Units** category that is marked as a "system" in the master catalog, ChairPulse displays a component selector. This lets you specify which sub-components are part of your particular installation.

For example, an A-Dec 500 system might include:
- Dental Chair
- Delivery Unit
- Operatory Light

Registering equipment as a system allows ChairPulse to scope maintenance tasks to specific components (e.g., "Lubricate chair hydraulics" targets the Dental Chair component, while "Clean fiber optic bundles" targets the Delivery Unit).

### Maintenance component types

When maintenance tasks are created for system equipment, they can be scoped to a specific component:

| Component Type | Label |
|---|---|
| `system_general` | General / Whole System |
| `dental_chair` | Dental Chair |
| `delivery_unit` | Delivery Unit |
| `operatory_light` | Operatory Light |

This scoping ensures maintenance checklists are specific and actionable for the technician performing the work.

## Compliance-relevant categories

Certain equipment categories trigger automatic compliance task creation when equipment is added. These tasks are based on your practice's jurisdiction and the applicable regulatory requirements. For example:

- **Autoclaves & Sterilizers** may generate spore testing and sterilization monitoring tasks.
- **X-Ray & Imaging** may generate radiation safety inspection and registration tasks.
- **Nitrous Oxide Systems** may generate sedation equipment inspection tasks.

See the [Compliance](/compliance) section for more details on how compliance tasks are managed.

## Custom categories

If your practice uses equipment that does not fit any of the 30 standard categories, you can create a custom category during the equipment setup wizard. Custom categories:

- Appear in filter dropdowns alongside standard categories
- Do not have pre-built maintenance recommendations from the master catalog (you will need to create custom maintenance schedules)
- Do not trigger automatic compliance task creation
