## Get your practice running on ChairPulse

ChairPulse keeps your dental equipment running and compliant by centralizing maintenance, AI diagnostics, and regulatory tasks for your practice.

<Callout kind="info">

### Before you begin

Have these details ready so you can complete setup in one sitting:

- A work email address you use at the practice
- Your practice name and street address
- Your state or province, so ChairPulse can load the right compliance rules
- Basic equipment info for at least one unit, such as:
  - A-Dec 500 treatment center (chair/operatory)
  - Midmark M11 sterilizer (autoclave)
  - Manufacturer, model number, and room or operatory label

Access ChairPulse at `https://app.chairpulse.com`.

</Callout>

## Complete the initial setup

Use this sequence to go from a new account to your first logged task.

<Steps>

<Step title="Create your account and log in" icon="rocket">

1. Go to `https://app.chairpulse.com`.
2. Select **Sign up** and enter:
   - Your name
   - Work email
   - A secure password

3. Check your inbox for the verification email from ChairPulse and follow the link to confirm your account.
4. After verification, sign in again using your email and password.

**Success check:** You land on the initial setup screen instead of being asked to verify your email again.

</Step>

<Step title="Enter practice details and jurisdiction" icon="home">

On the setup screen, enter your practice information so ChairPulse can load the correct compliance rules.

- Enter your **practice name** as patients and staff know it.
- Add your **street address**, city, and postal code.
- Select your **state or province** from the jurisdiction list.

ChairPulse uses your jurisdiction to auto-load compliance tasks, such as spore testing or waterline monitoring where required.

**Success check:** You see a confirmation or next-step screen indicating that compliance rules have been loaded for your location.

</Step>

<Step title="Add your first piece of equipment" icon="plus">

Start with one high-impact item, such as your primary chair or sterilizer.

1. Open the **Equipment** or **Inventory** section from the main navigation.
2. Select **Add equipment**.
3. Enter details such as:
   - **Category:** Chair, Sterilizer, Compressor, Vacuum, etc.
   - **Manufacturer:** For example, A-Dec, Midmark.
   - **Model:** For example, `A-Dec 500` or `Midmark M11`.
   - **Serial or asset ID:** From the manufacturer plate or your internal label.
   - **Location:** Room, operatory, or bay (for example, Operatory 3).

Add at least one item now; you can import or add more later.

**Success check:** Your equipment appears in the list with its name, model, and location visible.

</Step>

<Step title="Review auto-generated maintenance tasks and SOPs" icon="settings">

After you add equipment, ChairPulse generates maintenance tasks and standard operating procedures (SOPs) based on the models and your jurisdiction.

- Go to the **Tasks** or **Maintenance** section.
- Filter or search by the equipment you just added (for example, `Midmark M11`).
- Review the suggested:
  - Routine maintenance tasks (for example, weekly cleaning, monthly checks)
  - Compliance-related SOPs (for example, spore testing, waterline testing, where applicable)
  - Recommended frequencies and due dates

Use this pass to confirm that the schedule aligns with your current operations and regulatory expectations.

**Success check:** You see a list of tasks in statuses like `Logged` or `Scheduled`, with due dates tied to your equipment.

</Step>

<Step title="Run and log your first task" icon="check-circle">

Pick one straightforward task that is already due or due soon and use ChairPulse to track it end to end.

1. In the **Tasks** view, select a task for your newly added equipment.
2. Open the task to see its details and any linked SOP instructions.
3. Perform the steps on the operatory or sterilizer as described.
4. In ChairPulse, change the task status as you go:
   - From `Logged` or `Diagnosing` to `Scheduled` or `Visited` as appropriate
   - Mark it `Resolved` once the work is complete
5. Add a quick note or attachment if needed (for example, control strip result, checklist photo).

**Success check:** The task now shows as `Resolved` with your completion timestamp and notes recorded.

</Step>

<Step title="Optionally try AI diagnostics on an issue" icon="zap">

If you have a piece of equipment with a current or recurring issue, use diagnostics to test the workflow.

1. Go to the **Diagnostics** section or open the relevant equipment record.
2. Select **Run diagnostics**.
3. Capture clear photos that show:
   - Any error codes or lights on the control panel
   - The full face or side of the unit
   - Model and serial labels if visible
4. Upload the photos and submit for analysis.

ChairPulse generates an AI-assisted diagnostic report with confidence scores and recommended next steps, such as checks you can perform in-house or whether to schedule service.

**Success check:** You see a diagnostics report with a suggested cause and recommended actions for the issue.

</Step>

<Step title="Invite team members and assign tasks" icon="users">

Bring in teammates so responsibilities are clear and coverage is shared.

1. Open the **Users** or **Team** section from the main navigation.
2. Select **Invite user**.
3. Enter each teammate's:
   - Name
   - Work email
   - Role or permissions level (for example, Clinical Lead, Office Manager, Technician)
4. Send invitations and confirm they accept from their email.

After team members join:

- Open the **Tasks** view and assign recurring maintenance items to specific users or roles.
- Confirm that new assignments appear in each user's task list.

**Success check:** Invited users appear as active in the Users tab, and at least one recurring task shows an owner instead of being unassigned.

</Step>

</Steps>

## Advanced and optional setup

Use these sections when you are ready to go beyond the basics.

<Expandable title="Connect with other practice systems" default-open="false">

If your practice uses digital systems to track inventory, service tickets, or projects, you may want to connect ChairPulse so equipment and tasks stay in sync.

Common patterns include:

- Syncing asset IDs and locations from your asset or inventory system
- Mirroring maintenance tasks into a project or ticketing tool
- Linking ChairPulse task links into your internal SOP library

Check your practice's integration options (for example, IT-managed tools or existing APIs) and work with your technical contact to plan any connections. For deeper technical details, share the API Reference section of the docs with them.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Standardize SOPs across multiple locations" default-open="false">

If you oversee multiple locations, use ChairPulse to align SOPs while keeping jurisdiction-specific rules intact.

- Start from the auto-generated SOPs for a representative location.
- Adjust steps and notes to match your preferred clinical standards.
- Apply or copy those SOPs to other practices, then let ChairPulse layer in any local compliance differences.

This approach keeps maintenance consistent while honoring equipment and regulatory variations across sites.

</Expandable>

## If something does not look right

When tasks, diagnostics, or compliance rules seem off, use these checks to get back on track quickly.

- **Tasks look wrong for your equipment.** Confirm the model and jurisdiction:
  - Open the equipment record and verify the manufacturer and exact model (for example, `Midmark M11` vs another Midmark sterilizer).
  - Check that the practice address and state/province are correct in your settings.
- **Diagnostics recommendations do not match what you see.** Capture better evidence:
  - Take new photos with good lighting and sharp focus on error codes and panels.
  - Include at least one photo that clearly shows the model label.
  - Run diagnostics again and compare results.

If an issue persists or you are unsure how to act on a diagnostic suggestion:

- Review the [Diagnostics](/diagnostics) page for deeper guidance on evidence quality and interpreting reports.
- Visit the [Help Center](/help-center) for troubleshooting articles, including:
  - [Getting Started FAQ](/help-center/faq/getting-started)
  - [Cannot Invite User](/help-center/troubleshooting/can-not-invite-user)

For operational questions that are not covered there, use your preferred support channel from the Help Center.
